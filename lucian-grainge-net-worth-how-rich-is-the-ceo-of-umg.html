Lucian Grainge Net Worth & Career

In 2002, Grainge was appointed chairman of Universal Music Group UK, and in 2005, he was appointed chairman of Universal Music Group International. He kept it until 2010. On January 1, 2011, he succeeded Doug Morris as CEO of Universal Music Group.

In his first year as CEO, Universal purchased EMI’s Recorded Music division. According to Grainge, selling to Universal was the only way to improve EMI. The company then attempted to resurrect EMI’s defunct labels Capitol and Virgin. “Grainge will go down as the master architect of what is likely to be the last big deal in the music business,” Buzzfeed said of the EMI agreement.

Grainge has worked to expand Universal’s foreign digital expansion by forming distribution partnerships with companies such as Apple, Spotify, Facebook, Tencent, and YouTube. Grainge was the driving force behind the multi-year global license agreement between UMG and Spotify in 2017.

Grainge acquired Bravado, which was previously part of Sanctuary, on behalf of UMG and rebranded it as Bravado. Companies such as Barneys, Bloomingdales, and Selfridges have collaborated with the company to represent musicians such as Justin Bieber and Lady Gaga.

Grainge oversaw a global strategic collaboration with Live Nation for classical labels Deutsche Grammophon and Decca Classics.

In 2017, UMG was valued at $22 billion, more than treble what it was when Grainge took over.

In July 2018, JPMorgan estimated UMG’s value to be $40 billion, rising to $50 billion in 2019. Vivendi announced in 2015 that Grainge had agreed to continue as chairman and CEO of UMG until at least 2020. UMG increased annual sales, digital music revenue, and EBITDA under his leadership.
